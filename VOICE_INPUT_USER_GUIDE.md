# Voice Input User Guide

## Quick Start

### For Interview Questions
1. Click the **"Use Voice"** button next to any question
2. Click **"Start Recording"** when ready
3. Speak your answer clearly into your microphone
4. Click **"Stop Recording"** when finished
5. Review your recording using the audio player
6. Click **"Use This Recording"** to confirm, or **"Re-record"** to try again
7. Submit your interview when all questions are answered

### For Task Solutions
1. Open any task from your work dashboard
2. Click the **"Use Voice"** button instead of typing
3. Follow the same recording process as interviews
4. Submit your voice solution for grading

## Tips for Best Results

### Recording Quality
- **Speak clearly** and at a normal pace
- **Minimize background noise** (close windows, turn off fans)
- **Use a good microphone** if available (headset recommended)
- **Stay close to the microphone** (6-12 inches)
- **Avoid long pauses** (the system may think you're done)

### Answer Length
- **Aim for 30-60 seconds** for interview questions
- **Provide specific details** and examples
- **Answer all parts** of the question
- **Don't rush** - quality over speed

### Content Guidelines
- **Be professional** in your language
- **Stay on topic** - answer the question asked
- **Use technical terms** when appropriate
- **Explain your reasoning** - don't just state facts
- **Give examples** from your experience

## Common Issues

### "Microphone Not Found"
- **Check browser permissions**: Allow microphone access
- **Check system settings**: Ensure microphone is enabled
- **Try another browser**: Chrome/Firefox work best
- **Restart browser**: Sometimes permissions need a refresh

### "Recording Failed"
- **Check internet connection**: Voice requires upload
- **Try shorter recording**: Keep under 3 minutes
- **Clear browser cache**: Old data may cause issues
- **Use supported format**: WebM is recommended

### "Low Score on Voice Answer"
- **Speak more clearly**: Enunciate words
- **Add more detail**: Expand on your points
- **Answer the question**: Stay focused on what's asked
- **Check transcription**: If available, see what was heard

## Privacy & Security

### What We Record
- Only the audio you explicitly record
- Recording only happens when you click "Start Recording"
- You can review before submitting

### What We Don't Store
- Audio files are **not saved** permanently
- Temporary files are **deleted immediately** after processing
- Only the **transcription** is kept for grading

### Your Control
- You can **remove** voice answers before submitting
- You can **switch to text** at any time
- You can **re-record** unlimited times

## Grading Information

### How Voice Answers Are Graded
- **Same criteria** as text answers
- **Transcription** is evaluated by AI
- **No penalty** for using voice vs text
- **Fair scoring** based on content quality

### What Gets Good Scores (70+)
- Clear, detailed explanations
- Relevant technical knowledge
- Specific examples and reasoning
- Complete answers to all parts
- Professional communication

### What Gets Low Scores (< 70)
- Gibberish or unclear speech
- Very short answers (< 20 words)
- Off-topic responses
- Missing key concepts
- Vague or generic statements

## Troubleshooting

### Recording Doesn't Start
1. Check microphone is plugged in
2. Allow browser microphone permission
3. Refresh the page
4. Try a different browser

### Can't Hear Playback
1. Check system volume
2. Check browser isn't muted
3. Try headphones
4. Refresh the page

### Submission Takes Too Long
1. Check internet connection
2. Wait up to 90 seconds (normal for voice)
3. Don't refresh during submission
4. Contact support if it times out

### Transcription Seems Wrong
1. Speak more clearly next time
2. Reduce background noise
3. Use a better microphone
4. Consider typing instead for technical terms

## Best Practices

### When to Use Voice
- ✅ Interview questions (more natural)
- ✅ Explaining complex concepts
- ✅ Describing experiences
- ✅ Demonstrating communication skills
- ✅ When you prefer speaking over typing

### When to Use Text
- ✅ Code snippets or technical syntax
- ✅ Precise formatting needed
- ✅ In noisy environments
- ✅ When microphone unavailable
- ✅ For very short answers

### Mixing Voice and Text
- You can use **both** in the same interview
- Answer some questions with voice
- Answer others with text
- Choose what works best for each question

## Advanced Tips

### For Technical Interviews
- Spell out acronyms first time: "S-Q-L, or SQL"
- Pause briefly between concepts
- Use phrases like "for example" to structure answers
- Mention specific technologies clearly

### For Behavioral Questions
- Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Speak in complete sentences
- Provide context before diving into details
- End with a clear conclusion

### For Task Solutions
- Explain your approach first
- Walk through your solution step-by-step
- Mention any assumptions
- Summarize key points at the end
